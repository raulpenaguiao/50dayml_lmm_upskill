# Day 30: Project 3 - minGPT on Shakespeare (Portfolio Project) - Further Reading

This day synthesizes all Phase 3 concepts into a comprehensive GPT implementation project. Here are resources for building professional-quality language models and presenting your work.

## References

1. **The Complete Guide to Building Language Models**
   - [Hugging Face Course: Natural Language Processing](https://huggingface.co/course/)
   - Comprehensive course covering model building, training, evaluation, and deployment.

2. **Making Fast Language Models Faster with `flash-attention`**
   - [Dao et al.: Flash Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.14135)
   - Paper on efficient attention implementation for faster training, optional for production optimization.

3. **Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models**
   - [Kaplan et al.: Scaling Laws](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361)
   - Important paper on understanding how model size, data size, and compute scale with performance.

4. **Responsible AI and Language Model Evaluation**
   - [Bender et al.: On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3442188.3445922)
   - Critical perspective on limitations and ethical considerations for large language models.

5. **Deploying Language Models to Production**
   - [TorchServe Documentation](https://pytorch.org/serve/)
   - Guide to serving PyTorch models efficiently in production environments.

---

**Tip:** Start with reference #1 for comprehensive guidance, #2 for optimization, #3 for understanding scaling, #4 for ethical considerations, and #5 for deployment.
